Knowledge without consensus

Every Wikipedia article converges to a single version. But who decides what stays and what gets deleted?

Larry Sanger, Wikipedia's co-founder, now warns that the platform he helped create may be "entirely controlled by the CIA." Conservative sources are blacklisted. Dissenting views are systematically removed.

Wikifreedia takes a different approach. Built on Nostr, every article exists as a cryptographically signed event. There is no canonical version. No central authority.

Instead of forcing consensus, we embrace plurality. Your social graph becomes your editorial filter.

Bitcoin: A Peer-to-Peer Electronic Cash System

Commerce on the Internet has come to rely almost exclusively on financial institutions serving as trusted third parties to process electronic payments. While the system works well enough for most transactions, it still suffers from the inherent weaknesses of the trust based model.
What is needed is an electronic payment system based on cryptographic proof instead of trust, allowing any two willing parties to transact directly with each other without the need for a trusted third party. Transactions that are computationally impractical to reverse would protect sellers from fraud, and routine escrow mechanisms could easily be implemented to protect buyers.

Bitcoin: The Long Crypto Winter

Bitcoin was supposed to replace fiat currency, freeing us from the tyranny of central banks. More than a decade later, it remains too volatile for everyday transactions, too slow for actual payments, and too energy-intensive to scale globally.
The blockchain technology underlying Bitcoin may find legitimate uses in supply chain management or digital rights. But as currency—Bitcoin's original and central promise—it has failed. The dream of a decentralized monetary system has given way to speculative mania and environmental destruction.

Bitcoin: A Tool for Freedom

In authoritarian regimes where bank accounts can be frozen on government whim and assets seized without due process, Bitcoin provides an uncensorable alternative. From protesters in Nigeria being cut off from the banking system to dissidents in Russia watching their accounts disappear, cryptocurrency has become more than investment—it's survival.
This isn't about speculation or getting rich. It's about maintaining financial autonomy when your government turns against you. Bitcoin gives people a lifeline when traditional institutions become tools of oppression. Critics who dismiss it as a failed currency miss the point entirely.
