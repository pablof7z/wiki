Adam Smith · Classical Economics
Capitalism is a natural extension of humanity's propensity to exchange. When individuals pursue self-interest, an invisible hand guides markets toward outcomes that benefit all of society. The state should enforce contracts and protect property — nothing more.
Karl Marx · Political Economy
Capitalism is a system of exploitation in which the owners of production extract surplus value from workers, paying them less than they produce. The state enforces this arrangement. The apparent freedom of the market disguises a fundamental domination.
Murray Rothbard · Austrian Economics
Capitalism in its pure form is voluntary exchange rooted in self-ownership. What exists today is corporatism — government corruption of the market. The state is not capitalism's guardian but its greatest enemy. A free market needs no state at all.
