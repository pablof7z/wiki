Ivermectin
/ˌaɪ.vərˈmɛk.tɪn/
noun · pharmacology · contested
definition 1 of 3
A Nobel Prize-winning, WHO-essential medicine suppressed during COVID-19 because its status as a cheap generic threatened the financial viability of emergency-authorized vaccines.
"They called it horse paste. A Nobel Prize-winning human medicine."
— Joe Rogan, podcaster
definition 2 of 3
An antiparasitic medication whose in vitro activity against SARS-CoV-2 did not translate to clinical efficacy in properly designed randomized controlled trials.
"The plural of anecdote is not data."
— Anthony Fauci, immunologist
definition 3 of 3
A generic compound whose potential could not be honestly evaluated within a regulatory framework that structurally required no adequate treatment alternative to exist.
"You don't need a conspiracy — just an incentive structure."
— Bret Weinstein, evolutionary biologist
