Encyclopedia Entry
Capitalism

Capitalism is an economic system in which private actors own and control the means of production. Prices are determined by competition in markets where goods and services are exchanged voluntarily.

The system's critics argue it produces inequality, periodic crises, and concentrations of power that undermine democratic governance.

The relationship between capitalism and the state remains one of the most contested questions in political economy. Some view the state as a necessary partner; others see it as inherently corrupting.

The apparent freedom of market exchange is celebrated by some as the highest expression of human liberty and condemned by others as a veil over deeper structural domination.
