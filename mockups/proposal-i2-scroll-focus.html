Ivermectin

Ivermectin is an important antiparasitic medication with a well-established safety profile for its approved indications. However, in vitro activity against a virus does not translate to clinical efficacy — hundreds of drugs kill viruses in a petri dish at high enough concentrations.

"The plural of anecdote is not data. Randomized, placebo-controlled clinical trials are the gold standard, and they have not demonstrated significant benefit for COVID-19."

The TOGETHER trial, the largest well-designed RCT, showed no meaningful clinical benefit. When physicians promote unproven therapies based on personal experience and observational data, they undermine the evidentiary standards that protect patients from harm.

Promoting ivermectin as an alternative eroded confidence in vaccines — interventions that went through rigorous Phase III trials with tens of thousands of participants and demonstrably saved millions of lives worldwide.

Now someone who thinks both of them are missing the real question entirely.

Ivermectin

The ivermectin debate is actually a debate about something much larger: whether our regulatory and scientific institutions are structurally capable of honest evaluation when billions of dollars are at stake.

"The EUA framework legally required that 'no adequate, approved, available alternative' exist. This created a structural incentive to dismiss any early treatment — the entire vaccine program depended on it."

This isn't a conspiracy — it's an incentive structure. You don't need anyone in a room plotting. You just need a system where the most profitable outcome and the "scientific consensus" are required to align, and where anyone who questions that alignment gets professionally destroyed.

Both "miracle cure" and "horse paste" are wrong. The real question is: did we build a system capable of honestly evaluating a cheap generic drug when the most lucrative product in pharmaceutical history required that no such drug exist? The answer is obviously no.
